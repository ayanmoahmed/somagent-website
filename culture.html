Cultural activities consist primarily of poetry, folk dancing, the performance of plays, and singing. These traditional activities still retain their importance, especially in rural areas, and are practiced not only at family and religious celebrations but also at state ceremonies.

Hawo Ahmed Yussuf (Hawo-tako) was born in the countryside of Fafan District in the Somali Regional State of Ethiopia, around 1920s. She was born in a place called Fardo which is in the vicinity of Kabri Bayah town. She hailed from the Abasgul sub-clan of the Jidwak clan and her family was a pastoral nomads that used to rear their livestock and produce crops from their local farms. Her mother was from the Mohamed Zubeir sub-clan of the Ogaden clan, especially the Rer Isak micro family clan. The name of her mother was Amina Farah Hirsi from Dagahbur.
